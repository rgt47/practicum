# Editorial Review: Biostatistics Practicum
*2026-04-26 19:30 PDT*

## 1. Verdict

The practicum is the most mature of the four volumes in
the series. The editorial discipline is visible: 'Sources'
callouts at the head of every chapter cite the underlying
blog posts; 'Prerequisites' quizzes with answer keys appear
consistently; the 'statistician's contribution' template is
applied without feeling templated. The technical content
sampled is substantively correct and contemporary. The
weakest aspects relate to (a) tooling currency, where some
recipes encode 2024-era patterns now slightly behind
current best practice, (b) chapter-length variance (Ch 25
synthesis at 234 lines vs Ch 17 missing at 665), and (c) a
small number of cross-chapter consistency tics. None
threatens the book's substance; the volume is ready for
release after a focused tooling-currency pass.

## 2. Content-review findings

1. **Ch 17 missing-data, line 8: April 2026 syllabi survey
   citation needs the underlying file.** The chapter cites
   'a 14-of-22 finding' but no `docs/syllabi-survey.md`
   exists in the practicum repo. Either add the survey
   document or rephrase to the SCAI-advanced pattern
   ('survey of programmes documented in companion volume').

2. **Ch 21 AI-coding, lines 386-406, the 'where this book
   stands' meta-section.** The 70%/30% disclosure is
   honest but reads as the author's voice intruding into
   what is otherwise a textbook. Consider moving to a
   colophon or preface; in chapter 21 it tonally
   competes with the chapter content.

3. **Ch 25 synthesis is short (234 lines).** Compared to
   the closing-chapter weight in the sister volumes, this
   reads thin. Consider expanding to revisit the four
   parts of the book and to articulate the workflow as a
   pipeline rather than a checklist.

4. **Causal-inference is omitted; missingness covered.**
   Unlike SCAI-advanced (which omits causal-inference
   explicitly with disclosed rationale), the practicum
   does not declare its own scope omissions. A
   `00-intro.qmd` or `preface.qmd` paragraph would help
   a reader orient.

5. **Federal/regulatory context (Ch 2) and team-science
   (Ch 3) bookend Ch 1's 'why reproducibility' but
   their integration is implicit.** A short closing
   section in Ch 3 connecting back to Ch 1's
   motivations would close the part-1 loop.

6. **Ch 22 SAS treatment.** SAS is included for the
   regulatory-submission audience. Spot-check: is the
   chapter framed as 'when you must use SAS' or 'how
   to use SAS well'? The framing matters for the
   intended audience.

7. **AI-coding chapter does not catalogue Claude Code
   skills, hooks, or MCP integrations.** Given the
   2026 date, the absence of MCP/hooks/skills coverage
   in Ch 21 is a noticeable gap relative to current
   practice. A short subsection (or pointer to applied-
   genai Ch 6) is warranted.

8. **The book's own AI assistance is not documented in
   the methods/colophon.** Ch 21 line 388-406 mentions
   it; consider a single-page disclosure in the
   colophon documenting the verification regime applied
   during the book's development.

9. **CDISC chapter (Ch 19) currency.** SDTM and ADaM
   models drift; verify the chapter's references to
   current versions of the IGs.

10. **Ch 23-24 case studies (penguins, ADNI).** The
    pedagogical pairing is good; verify that the ADNI
    chapter respects current ADNI data-use restrictions
    (no patient-level data shown without confirmation).

11. **'Reproducible Reporting' part has only two
    chapters (11-12).** Compared to the five-chapter
    'Wrangling and Graphics' part, the reporting part
    feels under-weighted. Consider an additional
    chapter on bookdown/Quarto-book workflows for
    longer documents.

## 3. Technical findings

1. **Ch 21 line 198-202: the dplyr-1.1.0 `relationship`
   argument claim.** Correct for `dplyr 1.1.0+` but the
   argument was added specifically for `*_join`
   functions, not all dplyr operations. Tighten the
   wording.

2. **Ch 9 line 187-189: `renv` 1.0.7 is pinned.** As of
   April 2026 the current `renv` is 1.0.11+. The
   pinning pattern is sound; the example version may
   be stale. Consider using a `renv` env-var to make
   the version configurable.

3. **Ch 9 line 184: the apt-get install list does not
   include `libxml2-utils` or `libcurl4-openssl-dev`'s
   companions sometimes needed by `httr2`/`curl` on
   recent Debian.** Worth verifying against current
   `rocker` recommendations.

4. **Ch 9 line 372-380: Buildx multi-arch claim.** The
   `--platform linux/amd64,linux/arm64` flag requires
   Buildx setup (`docker buildx create --use`). Add a
   one-line setup hint.

5. **Ch 21 line 110: `chat_claude(model = "claude-sonnet-4-5")`.**
   `claude-sonnet-4-5` is current as of April 2026
   but will date. Consider a placeholder like
   `Sys.getenv("CLAUDE_MODEL", "claude-sonnet-4-5")`
   or annotate as 'as of 2026'.

6. **Ch 17 missing-data: `mice` model specifications.**
   Verify the `predictive mean matching` and `logistic
   regression` defaults in current `mice` (last major
   update touched some defaults). Spot-check.

7. **Ch 21 line 174-220: the failure-modes catalogue**
   is good but lacks 'silent dependency drift' (LLM
   suggests a package combination that worked in its
   training but conflicts now). Add this case.

8. **Bibliography count: 34 entries.** Run a key audit
   to confirm chapters do not cite missing keys. (The
   sister volume SCAI-advanced has the same problem at
   scale; verify practicum is not similarly affected.)

9. **Ch 21 line 250-260: `summarise_groups` example.**
   Correctly framed as hallucinated. Consider noting
   that the modern dplyr equivalent is
   `summarise(.by = grp, ...)` (added in dplyr 1.1.0)
   to make the answer fully current.

10. **Ch 9 line 273-281: `docker save -o ... .tar` plus
    Zenodo upload.** Correct for archival; worth
    noting the OCI-image-tar standard
    (`docker save --format=oci`) for forward
    compatibility.

11. **Ch 9 line 324-335: docker-compose v2 vs v1.**
    `docker-compose` (hyphenated) is being deprecated
    in favour of `docker compose` (subcommand). Update
    to the subcommand form.

## 4. Copy-edit findings

1. **Em-dashes used throughout, against user style
   guidance.** Sample: Ch 9 line 49 'For analyses that
   must reproduce exactly years later, or that depend
   on tricky system libraries (geospatial, GDAL, BLAS
   variants, JAGS, Stan), Docker is the right tool.'
   Numerous instances; lint pass needed.

2. **Inconsistent hyphenation: 'self-contained',
   'self contained', 'selfcontained' across chapters.**

3. **Tone: Ch 21 line 47-52 'This is a new fact, and
   it deserves an explicit response' is conversational
   in a way that diverges from the more measured tone
   elsewhere.** Consider a more academic phrasing.

4. **'Sources' callouts vary in detail.** Some cite
   blog post numbers (`46-ellmerinRcoding`); others
   cite by topic. Standardise.

5. **Ch 9 line 92: 'These judgements are what makes
   Docker a reproducibility tool rather than a slow
   build system.'** Subject-verb: 'are what *make*'.

6. **'Prerequisites' quiz answers at chapter end.** A
   pedagogically smart device but inconsistently
   numbered (`@sec-quiz-answers-21` works; verify
   anchors elsewhere).

7. **Three-judgement formula appears in Ch 9 ('three
   judgements'), Ch 17, Ch 21.** The formula is
   shared with SCAI-advanced and applied-genai.
   Consistent across the series; safe to keep.

8. **Code-block fencing.** Mostly ` ```r `, ` ```bash `,
   ` ```dockerfile `. Consistent. Good.

9. **Ch 21 line 174: 'A non-exhaustive catalogue'.**
   Stylistic preference: 'A partial catalogue' reads
   slightly more confidently.

10. **Cross-volume voice consistency.** Read against
    SCAI-advanced and applied-genai: practicum is
    less formal, more 'how to' than 'why'. Appropriate
    for a workflow book; the difference is intentional.
